## Design Context

### Users
Organizational leaders, HR directors, and transformation teams across Africa — primarily in Kenya and East Africa. They encounter this site during business hours on desktop/tablet while researching organizational development consultants. They're navigating complex change (mergers, leadership transitions, culture shifts) and need a partner who feels trustworthy, grounded, and experienced — not flashy or corporate.

### Brand Personality
**Patient, Wise, Warm** — like the Baobab tree itself. The brand embodies deep-rooted strength, adaptive intelligence, and communal generosity. It is confident without being loud, editorial without being cold, and African without being decorative.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Earthy Editorial** — a warm, grounded editorial style with rounded UI elements. Inspired by premium print magazines and thoughtful nature photography, not tech startups or corporate consulting firms.

- **Typography**: Source Serif 4 (headlines) + Figtree (body). Strong serif/sans contrast.
- **Color**: OKLCH palette centered on earth tones — baobab-earth (deep brown), warm-sand, growth-green, resilience-clay (terracotta). Tinted neutrals, never pure gray.
- **Layout**: Left-aligned editorial flow with asymmetric compositions. Numbered divider lists over card grids. Generous whitespace between sections.
- **Theme**: Light mode primary — the audience browses during business hours. Dark sections used sparingly for emphasis (The Empty Center statement).
- **Rounded UI**: `border-radius: 0.625rem` (rounded-md) applied consistently to cards, buttons, tables, containers.

**Anti-references**: Generic corporate consulting sites, dark-mode SaaS dashboards, cyan/purple gradient aesthetics, glassmorphism.

### Design Principles
1. **Grounded before flashy** — Every design choice should reinforce trust and warmth. Drama comes from typography and space, not effects.
2. **Editorial confidence** — Treat content like a magazine spread: strong headlines, pull quotes, generous margins, intentional hierarchy.
3. **Rhythm over uniformity** — Alternate tight groupings with generous breathing room. No two adjacent sections should feel the same.
4. **Motion with purpose** — Animations reveal content and provide feedback, never decorate. Smooth exponential easing, never bounce.
5. **African warmth, global quality** — The palette and tone reflect African earth and community. The craft reflects world-class design standards.
